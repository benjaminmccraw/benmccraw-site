04 · Phased Delivery

Two phases to a connected estimator

Phase 1 · Finish the field estimator

A complete, on-site estimating tool your reps use end to end.

1

Discovery & experience refinements

As part of finishing the build, we review how your inside and field sales teams actually work and fold in targeted refinements so the tool fits your process and stays intuitive for every user. This is what makes the difference between a tool that is merely complete and one your team is glad to use every day.

2

Complete estimates workflow

Complete the guided estimate flow: select material, style, height, and color, draw the fence, and produce a price on-site, with the validation that keeps every required input correct. Includes the admin screen so your team can manage products and prices yourselves.

3

Automatic bill of materials

Complete the materials list for every product, not just Lakeland: real part numbers and pricing, so the bill of materials is accurate and ready to become an order.

4

Picture storage

Finish and verify photo capture, phone upload, and markup so every job carries its site photos.

5

Customer signatures · new

Capture the customer's signature on the quote, right on the device, so an approved quote is documented.

6

Lead / status tracking · new

Every quote carries a status, a follow-up date, and the rep it belongs to, so nothing slips.

Draft→Sent→Signed / Won→Lost

On lead tracking and NetSuite: this runs inside the tool on its own and does not require NetSuite. Phase 2 then syncs it to NetSuite so the two systems stay in step automatically.

Also included in Phase 1:

  • Renewing the lapsed security certificate and hardening the environment
  • The admin screen to manage products and prices
  • Loading your product line and pricing, from data you provide
  • Testing on real devices in the field
  • Training your team
  • Timeline ~6–8 weeks, with data-gathering running in parallel so the build is not waiting

Phase 2 · Connect to NetSuite

The piece that removes the hand re-keying.

  • When a rep saves a quote, it creates or updates the Opportunity in NetSuite automatically (customer, address, quote).
  • When the customer signs, the tool creates the Sales Order and pushes the bill of materials into NetSuite for work orders and purchasing.
  • Lead/status tracking becomes two-way, with NetSuite as the system of record, so there is no double entry.

This is the most specialized work, and the exact effort depends on your NetSuite setup (how it is accessed, who administers it, whether there is a test environment). Phase 2 is confirmed once we have reviewed your NetSuite with your administrator. Multi-entity support, if needed, is scoped at that point.

05 · Beyond Phase 2

Your continuing roadmap

Your requirements document describes a complete estimating-and-sales platform. Phases 1 and 2 focus on building the foundation of that platform; the remaining features are addressed over time, through future phases or ongoing support:

  • Commercial estimating: plan uploads, digital takeoffs, bid packages

Starting with this foundational work puts a working system in your team's hands quickly, and gives you a stable base to keep building toward the full vision, one phase at a time, with each step delivering value of its own.
